I am a feminist social scientist with a focus on American politics, political behavior, and quantitative methods. My research interests broadly encompass campaigns and elections, with a particular emphasis on gender and voter behavior. I’m especially interested in voter responses to women and members of other marginalized groups as electoral candidates. My dissertation project explores the role of patriarchal gender norms in shaping women's political pathways.

I hold a BS in Social Policy from Northwestern University, an MA in Political Science from the University of Chicago, and a graduate certificate in Gender, Leadership, and Public Policy from the University of Massachusetts—Boston. I worked in Democratic fundraising for several years before coming to Berkeley, and my work has been published in the Los Angeles Times and Political Behavior.
